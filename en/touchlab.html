Touchlab: Tactile AI Roadmap

Click on each stage to see how "e-skin" technology scales

1. Problem Discovery (University Origins) +
  • Funding Academic grants and University of Edinburgh research support.
  • Strategic Moves Identifying that while robots have "vision" and "hearing," they lack a human-like sense of "touch," which limits their ability to handle fragile objects.
  • Cost Structure Research personnel and laboratory equipment.
2. Solution Exploration +
  • Funding Pre-seed funding and innovation competitions.
  • Strategic Moves Developing the first "electronic skin" (e-skin) prototype that is thinner than human skin but can withstand extreme temperatures and pressures.
  • Revenue Streams Grant-funded pilot projects and university partnerships.
3. Product–Market Fit +
  • Funding Early Venture Capital and Angel investors.
  • Strategic Moves Pivoting from general robotics to specific high-value sectors like healthcare (tele-operated surgery) and nuclear decommissioning where haptic feedback is critical.
  • Cost Structure Engineering talent and initial patent filings.
4. Early Operations & Pilot Testing +
  • Funding Seed Round.
  • Strategic Moves Deploying "Telerobot" pilots where doctors can "feel" patients from miles away. Testing the durability of sensors in industrial environments.
  • Revenue Streams Paid pilot programs and Proof of Concepts (PoCs).
5. Scaling (Current Phase) +
  • Funding Series A.
  • Strategic Moves Integrating tactile sensors into standard industrial robotic arms (Universal Robots, KUKA). Scaling manufacturing of the e-skin material.
  • Cost Structure Sales & Marketing, large-scale manufacturing facilities, and AI data training for touch patterns.
6. Maturity & Transformation +
  • Strategic Moves Becoming the global standard for robotic "skin." Moving into consumer robotics and smart prosthetics.
  • Revenue Streams Licensing the technology to major robot manufacturers and subscription-based "Haptics-as-a-Service."
